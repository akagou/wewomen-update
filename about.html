Our mission

We started asking questions. We couldn't stop.

For too long, women's health has been an afterthought. Research has historically focused on men. Conversations about what happens to a woman's body after 35 have been hushed, rushed, or skipped entirely. And the result? Millions of women navigating real, significant changes with almost no reliable guidance.

Not because the information doesn't exist. Because nobody made it their job to give it to you.

That's what we're here to change.

Our story

It started with one conversation.

It started with a conversation about the lack of support for new mothers in the Netherlands — and quickly became something much bigger.

The more we looked, the clearer it became: women as a whole are left struggling and searching for answers as they move through their 30s, 40s and beyond. Experiencing real, significant changes in their bodies — and being met with dismissal, confusion, or silence.

We decided that wasn't good enough. So we built WeWomen.

What drives us

Add life to your years.

We're not interested in quick fixes or scare tactics. We're interested in giving women the kind of clear, honest, practical information that actually changes how they live.

There's a difference between lifespan and healthspan — and we care deeply about the second one. Things like bone density, muscle strength, pelvic floor health and hormonal balance don't have to decline the way most women expect. Small, consistent changes — built on real knowledge — make more difference than any crash programme ever will.

We're not here to add years to your life. We're here to add life to your years.
